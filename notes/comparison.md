# Comparison: DCS vs. DSS

## Decision Support System (DSS)
- **Focus:** Data-driven, often used in corporate settings.
- **Goal:** To provide a comprehensive view of data to help experts make complex decisions.
- **Inputs:** Large datasets, spreadsheets, or internal databases.
- **Output:** Reports, dashboards, and analytical models.

## Decision Companion System (DCS)
- **Focus:** Human-centric, often AI-powered.
- **Goal:** To be a partner in the decision-making process, focusing on logic, weight, and bias reduction.
- **Inputs:** Qualitative and quantitative criteria defined by the user for a specific decision.
- **Output:** Clear rankings, recommendations, and insights into the decision-making process itself.

## Key Difference
A DSS provides the **data** to make a decision, while a DCS provides the **logic and framework** to evaluate that data and make a choice.
